We started a CS theory club at Cornell! Find our website [here](https://theoryclub.cs.cornell.edu/). Professors and graduate students gave 9 talks introducing topics spanning across cryptography, algorithms, approximation algorithms, complexity, game theory, differential privacy, and more. 

As an undergraduate interested in CS theory, I was stuck in a hard place. Undergrads takes the classes and TA for it, but have no other organized way to meet up. So we founded an unofficial CS theory club, and are planning to register as an official organization next semester.

In the club, 15-25 students attend every week. We're happy to see each other and have a place to nerd out. Shout out to the CS department for covering food for every week's talk! And of course, thank you to the guidance of our advisor Eva Tardos. I also want to thank each of my E-board members: Ahan Misra, Lawrence Ozoigbo, Max Scialabba, Nancy Chen, Saket Murali, Shaleen Baral, and Sophia Wong.
